In social psychology, a stereotype is an over-generalized belief about a particular category of people. It is an expectation that people might have about every person of a particular group. The type of expectation can vary; it can be, for example, an expectation about the group's personality, preferences, or ability.The words stereotype or stereotyping, we don’t really deeply think about these words often, nor are they used very much in our daily vocabulary. We stereotype left to right; its just part of our nature. To stereotype a person is to put them under a certain title, or group them based upon how they act, what they look like, or sometimes based on things they cannot control like race.
